Part V · Collision Response · Chapter 18

Resting Contact & Sleeping

A box sitting on the ground looks like it's doing nothing. In fact it's being held up, every single frame, by a fresh volley of impulses fighting gravity. This final chapter makes that quiet — and then stops doing the work entirely once a body has clearly gone still, so a pile of hundreds can rest for free.

Goal

Understand resting contact, and add sleeping (with a nod to islands) so settled bodies cost nothing and stop their last jitter. Engine: physics/world.hpp. Demo: chapters/18-resting/main.cpp.

Resting contact is not "no contact"

Intuition Gravity pulls the box down a tiny bit every step; the contact solver pushes back with a normal impulse that exactly cancels it. The box appears still, but it's really a balance of two opposing streams of impulses — like standing still on a trampoline. This is why the machinery from the last two chapters is enough: a "resting" contact is just a contact whose closing speed the solver keeps driving to zero, frame after frame. Warm starting matters most here — re-using yesterday's supporting impulse is what keeps the balance smooth instead of buzzing.

The jitter problem, and slop

Perfectly resolving penetration every frame makes bodies twitch: they get pushed out, fall back in, get pushed out again. We calm this two ways: a small slop (we don't bother correcting overlaps shallower than a few millimetres) and a gentle Baumgarte factor (we correct only a fraction of the remaining depth each frame). Together they trade a sliver of visible overlap for a lot of stillness.

Sleeping: stop simulating what isn't moving

Even calmed, a settled body keeps consuming solver time forever. The fix is simple and powerful: if a body's linear and angular speeds stay below small thresholds for long enough, put it to sleep — skip integrating and solving it until something wakes it. A settled pile then costs almost nothing, and its final micro-motion vanishes.

Islands: sleep and wake together

Why touching bodies share a fate A body resting on a sleeping one must not sink through it, and a whole stack should nod off — and later wake — as a unit. Bodies connected by contacts form an island. Our rule is simple: a body in contact with an awake body is kept awake, so waking one member ripples through the island over the next few frames. (A production engine groups islands explicitly with union-find; the contact-propagation version is enough here.)

The check is just a speed test with a timer:

const Real lin = length(rbs[i].linearVelocity);
const Real ang = length(rbs[i].angularVelocity());
if (lin < linearSleepSpeed && ang < angularSleepSpeed) {
    sleepTimer[i] += dt;
    if (sleepTimer[i] > timeToSleep) {           // still for long enough → sleep
        awake[i] = 0;
        rbs[i].linearVelocity = {}; rbs[i].angularMomentum = {};   // freeze it cleanly
    }
} else { sleepTimer[i] = 0; awake[i] = 1; }        // moving → stay/return awake

The whole engine, in one step

With this, World::step is the entire course in six lines: gravity, broadphase (Ch 12), narrowphase (Ch 13–15), warm-started impulse solve (Ch 16–17), integrate positions (Ch 11), and sleep. Everything you've built, running together.

Run it

./build/bin/ch18_resting        # 1 pile settles & sleeps · 2 drop re-wakes · 3 sleeping OFF

Bodies rain into a bin. Awake ones glow amber; as they settle they turn dim blue and the HUD's awake-count falls toward zero. In scene 2, a fresh body dropped onto the sleeping pile wakes just the island it lands on.

Experiments

  • Press S (or use scene 3) to disable sleeping. The settled pile never fully stops — a permanent, faint shimmer of micro-jitter. Turn it back on and the pile goes truly still. That shimmer is the cost sleeping removes.
  • In scene 2, watch which bodies re-wake when a new one lands: only the ones it touches, and their neighbours a frame later — the island waking up.

Takeaways

  • A resting contact is an active balance of gravity and normal impulses, kept smooth by warm starting.
  • Slop and a gentle Baumgarte factor trade a little overlap for a lot of stillness.
  • Sleeping skips still bodies entirely; contact keeps an island awake and asleep together.
