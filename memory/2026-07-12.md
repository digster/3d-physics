# 2026-07-12 — Project kickoff & Milestone 1 (Part I)

## What this project is
A tutorial-based course for learning **3D physics** by building a small engine
from scratch in **SDL3 + C++20**. Each chapter = a runnable demo
(`chapters/NN-*/main.cpp`) + an in-depth static HTML doc page (`docs/`, no build
step). No physics or graphics engine dependency — we write the integrators,
collision, solver, and even a software 3D renderer ourselves.

Full plan: 20 chapters across 7 parts (Foundations → Particles → Rigid Bodies →
Collision Detection → Collision Response → Joints → Soft Bodies), delivered
**checkpoint per part**. Rendering is a **software pipeline** on
`SDL_RenderGeometry` (chosen so the 3D math is tutorial content, not a black box).

## Gotchas / learnings (see also LEARNINGS.md)
- Clang rejects `Config cfg = {}` default arg when the type has a `std::string`
  NSDMI *inside the enclosing class*; use a delegating default ctor instead.
- doctest's CMake needs `CMAKE_POLICY_VERSION_MINIMUM=3.5` under CMake 4.x.
- The in-app automated browser reports pages as `document.hidden`, which pauses
  `requestAnimationFrame` — widgets are fine for real users; to verify in the
  pane, patch rAF to `setTimeout`. Also the pane's screenshot compositor can go
  blank right after a *programmatic* scroll (scroll to top and it repaints).

### CRITICAL LEARNING — rotational energy leak (see LEARNINGS.md)
First rigid-body integrator used the naive quaternion step
`q += ½ ω⊗q · dt; normalize`. It conserved L exactly but **injected energy**: a
free spin sped up unboundedly (KE 11→21 over 10 s) instead of flipping — the
Chapter 5 explicit-Euler leak, in rotational form. Fixed with an **exponential-map
midpoint** step in `RigidBody::integrate` (sample ω at the step midpoint, rotate
by the exact angle |ω|·dt). Then KE holds steady and the Dzhanibekov flip appears
correctly. Verified by a standalone probe and the unit test. The flip's tell is
the *body-fixed spin axis reversing in world space* — NOT the world-frame ω_y
(which stays ~constant for the intermediate spin); don't test the wrong signal.

### CRITICAL LEARNING — GJK/EPA robustness (see LEARNINGS.md)
EPA is winding-sensitive. It diverged (hundreds of faces, no convergence) because
(a) the initial tetra face set wasn't consistently wound, and (b) `addFace` was
`std::swap`-ing indices to orient normals — which breaks the horizon-edge
cancellation. Fix: consistently-wound face set `{0,1,2},{0,3,1},{0,2,3},{1,3,2}`
and flip only the normal *vector*. Also: GJK's box simplex can be degenerate →
"blow up" to a real tetra before EPA. And EPA's outward normal is A→B, so negate
for the B→A Contact convention. All cross-checked against SAT.

### CRITICAL LEARNING — Baumgarte energy leak (see LEARNINGS.md)
Summing the restitution and Baumgarte biases made a dropped ball bounce HIGHER
than it fell (effective e ~0.96 for a set 0.8) — deep single-step penetration
inflates the baumgarte term on top of the real bounce. Fix: `max(restitution,
baumgarte)`. Also: clamp the *accumulated* normal impulse (not the delta);
spawn bodies one-at-a-time to avoid overlap-launch; test stacks with sleeping ON
(basic sequential impulse drifts on tall stacks otherwise).

### CRITICAL LEARNING — jointed bodies must not collide (see LEARNINGS.md)
The hinge went berserk (ω spiked to thousands, door locked horizontal) NOT because
of joint math but because the door and frame colliders overlapped at the pivot and
the contact solver fought the joint. Fix: exempt connected pairs from collision.
Also: a hinge is 3+2 constraints (two ball-sockets = 6, over-constrained & fight);
test undamped joint pendulums on the swing extremum, not a final snapshot.

### Learnings (see LEARNINGS.md)
XPBD compliance is very non-linear — sweep it (α≈0.02 is good jello; 0.2 collapses).
A soft cube needs volume (body-diagonal) constraints, not just edges + shear.
